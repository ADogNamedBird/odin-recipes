Eggs Benedict

Description

Eggs Benedict is a classic and indulgent brunch dish that never fails to impress with its perfect combination of rich flavors and luxurious textures. This iconic dish typically consists of a toasted English muffin topped with Canadian bacon or ham, a perfectly poached egg, and a generous drizzle of velvety hollandaise sauce. The magic lies in the contrast between the soft, runny yolk of the poached egg and the buttery richness of the hollandaise sauce, creating a symphony of flavors that dance on the palate. Each component is carefully crafted and assembled, resulting in a culinary masterpiece that elevates the brunch experience to new heights.

To recreate this culinary delight at home, start by toasting split English muffins and topping each half with a slice of Canadian bacon or ham. The perfectly poached eggs, with their runny yolks, are then delicately placed on top. The pièce de résistance is the hollandaise sauce—a smooth and buttery emulsion of egg yolks, lemon juice, and melted butter. This luscious sauce is drizzled generously over the eggs, adding a decadent finish to the dish. With its harmonious blend of textures and flavors, Eggs Benedict remains a timeless favorite for those seeking a sophisticated and indulgent brunch experience.

Ingredients

  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1-2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice
  • salt to taste
  • pinch cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the Hollandaise Sauce: In a heatproof bowl, whisk together 3 large egg yolks and 1 tablespoon of water. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and continue to whisk until the yolks start to thicken. Slowly drizzle in 1 cup of melted unsalted butter while whisking continuously until the sauce is smooth and thickened. Add 1-2 teaspoons of fresh lemon juice, salt, and a pinch of cayenne pepper for flavor. Keep the hollandaise warm over the double boiler, stirring occasionally.
  2. Poach the Eggs: Bring a pot of water to a gentle simmer. Add a splash of white vinegar. Crack each egg into a small bowl. Create a gentle whirlpool in the simmering water and carefully slide the eggs, one at a time, into the center of the whirlpool. Poach for about 3-4 minutes for a runny yolk. Use a slotted spoon to lift the poached eggs from the water, allowing excess water to drain.
  3. Prepare the English Muffins and Canadian Bacon: Toast English muffin halves until golden brown. In a separate pan, lightly brown Canadian bacon or ham slices.
  4. Assemble: Place the toasted English muffin halves on a plate. Top each half with a slice of Canadian bacon or ham. Carefully place a poached egg on each muffin half.
  5. Drizzle with Hollandaise: Spoon the warm hollandaise sauce generously over each poached egg, ensuring it covers the entire surface. The rich, velvety sauce will cascade over the eggs, creating a decadent finish.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Optionally, garnish with a sprinkle of chopped fresh chives, parsley, or a dash of paprika for added color and flavor. Serve immediately, and enjoy the luxurious experience of Eggs Benedict for a brunch to remember.
